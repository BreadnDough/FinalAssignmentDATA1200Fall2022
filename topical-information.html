Fake news and critical thinking in the digital era[1]

In our view, comedic news is not included in our definition of fake news. We don't believe any rational person would think comedic news counts as real news. Some irrational individuals will always be out there, believing an article from a comedic news site like theOnion is true. However, just because someone doesn't comprehend a joke doesn't mean it isn't a joke.

Secondly our definition fits with the intuitive cases of fake news where it identifies situations in which the media tries to disinform the audience. For instance, in 2016, ABC news reported a case where they disinformed their viewers[2]. The crew deliberately told inaccurate information about the tape was put there by the sheriff rather than put there by the media crew. Also, we need to point out, that it is a risk if our own media lacks accountability when covering news, then distrust in media will increase. This is worrisome because it can quickly poison the very fabric of our society.

Thirdly fake news does not necessitate deliberate deceit. In cases where reporters do not care about facts, but where there is no intention to deceive anybody, then they are misinforming its audience. If a reporter makes “bullshit” statement and wants to only appear more knowledgeable than they might be, then their news is fake regardless of whether they were trying to deliberately deceive you or not.

Finally, fakes news needs to resemble real news in such a way that it has the presentation of news that has a factual basis. Also, it’s required to aim to pass as real news to the extent that a somewhat rational person takes it to be real news. It is not sufficient that fake news is false, and it is not necessary that the media tries to deceive you.
